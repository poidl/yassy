1 Postﬁlter for BLIT sawtooth VA synth

TEst

1 0.65 + 0.35z−1

I’m trying to understand the postﬁlter for the bandlimited sawtooth VA synth described by (Frei, 2002, his page 17), deﬁned by

1 0.65 + 0.35z−1. (1)

This is an inﬁnite impulse response (IIR) ﬁlter with a single-pole. These ﬁlters are generally of the form (Wikipedia, 2016)

y[n] = 1 a0(b0x[n] − a1y[n − 1]). (2)

and have a transfer function deﬁned by

H(z) = b0 a0 + a1z−1. (3)

Eq. 2 and (3) can be written in the more ”traditional” (Wikipedia, 2016) forms

y[n] = b0′x[n] − a 1′y[n − 1]. (4)

and

H(z) = b0′ 1 + a1′z−1 (5)

by setting b0′ = b 0∕a0 and a1′ = a 1∕a0. Multiplying the previous line by z yields

H(z) = b0′z z + a1′, (6)

showing that H(z) has a zero at z = 0 and a pole at z = −a1′. Setting z = x + iy one obtains

Re(H(z)) = b0′ x2 + y2 + a 1′x x2 + y2 + 2a1′x + a1′ 2 (7) Im(H(z)) = b0′ a1′y x2 + y2 + 2a1′x + a1′ 2 (8)

The speciﬁc ﬁlter (1) is obtained by setting

b0′ = 1.54 (9) a1′ = 0.54, (10)

and Re(H(z)) is shown in Fig. 1.
